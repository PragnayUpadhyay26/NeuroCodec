01 — Problem

Why JPEG isn't enough

Static Transforms

JPEG uses fixed DCT — it cannot adapt its transform to image content. High-frequency textures and complex scenes are poorly represented.

Block Artefacts

8×8 block processing introduces visible ringing and blocking at low bitrates — a fundamental limitation of the DCT approach.

Neural Solution

Learned codecs use end-to-end optimised transforms and adaptive entropy models — the same bitrate, higher quality.

Literature Foundation

Based on Ballé et al. (2018) — "Variational image compression with a scale hyperprior" — which demonstrated that hyperprior-based neural codecs consistently outperform JPEG on the Kodak benchmark at low-to-mid bitrates.

Research Gap Addressed

Replaced fixed N(0,1) prior with learned EntropyBottleneck + GaussianConditional for accurate rate estimation.

Benchmark

Evaluated on the standard Kodak 24-image dataset — the same benchmark used in all published neural codec papers.

04 — Contributions

Key Improvements

01

GDN / IGDN Activations

Replaced ReLU with Generalised Divisive Normalisation — designed specifically for image statistics. Decorrelates latent channels better than ReLU, directly improving entropy coding efficiency.

02

Learned Entropy Models

Replaced fixed N(0,1) prior with CompressAI's EntropyBottleneck and GaussianConditional. Rate estimation is now accurate and differentiable end-to-end.

03

MS-SSIM Perceptual Loss

L1 loss produces blurry reconstructions — JPEG looks sharper because DCT preserves structure. MS-SSIM targets edges and textures directly, producing perceptually superior output at the same bpp.

04

Residual Decoder + Wider Channels

Added ResBlocks in the synthesis transform for better gradient flow. Increased channels from 64–96 to 128/192 — more capacity to represent fine-grained image detail.

Future Work

Larger Dataset

Train on DIV2K + Flickr2K (3,450 images) to match published results and close the JPEG gap.

Multi-λ RD Curve

Train 5 models at λ ∈ {0.001, 0.004, 0.016, 0.04, 0.08} to generate a full curve above JPEG.

Attention Mechanism

Add a self-attention layer at the bottleneck — Cheng et al. 2020 shows +0.5–1 dB gain on Kodak.
